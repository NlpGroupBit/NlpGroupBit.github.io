Biography

Yang Gao, Doctoral Supervisor, primarily engages in large language model training, automatic text generation technologies, and the application and transformation of these technologies. She has published over 60 high-quality papers in international journals and conferences, including ACL, AAAI, WWW, IJCAI, EMNLP, and TKDE. She serves as a chair in the text generation field for conferences such as EMNLP and COLING, an editorial board member for journals such as Web Intelligence and Natural Language Processing Journal, a program committee member for international conferences including AAAI, ACL, EMNLP, NAACL, and ICDM, and a reviewer for journals such as TNNLS and Computing Surveys. She leads the MindLLM project, which focuses on the development of indigenous large models and has open-sourced an “edge-side” dialogue large model. His work includes launching an intelligent government service enhanced by retrieval-augmented models. As a participant, She has received the First Prize of the Science and Technology Progress Award from the China Institute of Electronics and the Second Prize of the National Defense Science and Technology Progress Award. She has been a visiting scholar at the University of Edinburgh, the Deputy Secretary-General of the Natural Language Generation and Intelligent Writing Committee of the Chinese Information Processing Society of China (CIPSC), a member of the Young Scholars Committee of CIPSC, and an executive committee member of the CCF Large Model Forum.
